# Cutting-edge adult ADHD research: 2023–2026

*Source: Gemini research synthesis, April 2026*

---

## 1. The brain in ADHD: distributed disruption, not a single broken circuit

**Norman et al. (2024, *American Journal of Psychiatry*)** — largest resting-state fMRI mega-analysis (1,696 ADHD vs. 6,737 controls): heightened striatal-cortical connectivity, effect sizes just **d = 0.11–0.15**.

**Mooney et al. (2024, *Journal of Neuroscience*)** — "polyneuro risk score" from ABCD study (N = 5,543): cumulative brain-wide connectivity, not any single circuit, correlates with ADHD symptoms.

**Liu et al. (2024, *CNS Neuroscience & Therapeutics*)** — DMN hypoconnectivity + increased inter-network bridging in 84 adults with ADHD, linked to **MAOA and MAOB** variants. 12 weeks of CBT partially restored patterns.

**Glutamate emerging:**
- **Vidor et al. (2024)**: lower glutamate in posterior cingulate cortex in 88 adults with ADHD
- **Bouyssi-Kobar et al. (2026, *Translational Psychiatry*)**: glutamate developmental trajectory predicts ADHD persistence vs. remission

**Itagaki et al. (2024)**: drug-naïve SPECT — **reduced** (not elevated) DAT in nucleus accumbens, supporting reward system hypofunction.

**Novel biomarkers:**
- Retinal fundus photos: 95–97% AUROC (Choi et al., 2025)
- MRI brain charts: distinct neurobiological subtypes (Pecci-Terroba et al., 2025)

## 2. Pharmacology enters its most active decade

### New molecules

**Centanafadine** (Otsuka) — triple reuptake inhibitor (NE, DA, 5-HT). NDA submitted Nov 2025, **PDUFA July 24, 2026**. Significantly better tolerability than LDX, atomoxetine, viloxazine in indirect comparisons.

**CTx-1301** (Cingulate) — precision-timed dexmethylphenidate. **PDUFA May 31, 2026**.

**Arynta** — first liquid lisdexamfetamine, FDA approved June 2025.
**Onyda XR** — first liquid nonstimulant, approved October 2024.

### Landmark meta-analyses

**Ostinelli et al. (2025, *Lancet Psychiatry*)** — largest ever (>14,500 participants, 113 RCTs):
- Only **stimulants and atomoxetine** demonstrated beneficial effects at 12 weeks
- **No ADHD medication improved quality of life**
- Non-pharmacological approaches: concerning rater-discrepancy problem

**Kay et al. (2025, *Cell*)** — paradigm shift (fMRI, 5,795 children):
- Stimulants **primarily activate reward and wakefulness circuits, not attention networks**
- Changes mimicked effects of good sleep
- Implication: assess sleep deprivation before prescribing

### Safety

**Li et al. (2024, *JAMA*)**: ADHD medication = **21% lower all-cause mortality** (Swedish cohort, 148,578)
- 25% reduction in unnatural deaths (accidents, suicide, poisoning)

**Li et al. (2025, *JAMA Psychiatry*)**: protective associations weakened as prescription rates broadened

### Novel approaches: mostly negative

**Mueller et al. (2025, *JAMA Psychiatry*)** — first psychedelic microdosing RCT:
- LSD 20 μg twice weekly for 6 weeks
- **Not more efficacious than placebo**
- 80% treatment-guessing rate suggests expectancy effects

**GLP-1 agonists (Ozempic)**: no clinical trials for ADHD exist

**Cannabis**: only RCT (Cooper et al., 2017) showed no benefit; may worsen inattention

### Pharmacogenomics

**Zhao et al. (2025)**: NKAIN2 identified as predictor of treatment response
**iPSYCH study (2026)**: N = 18,362 on stimulant discontinuation genetics
CYP2D6 testing for atomoxetine dosing = only immediately actionable PGx

## 3. Genetics: from 27 loci to 56, plus rare variants

**Demontis et al. (2023, *Nature Genetics*)**: 27 loci, ~225,000 individuals

**Van der Laan, Ip, Schipper et al. (2025, *Nature Genetics*)**: 290,134 measures, 70,953 individuals
- **39 independent loci (17 new)**
- 22 potential ADHD effector genes via FLAME method

**Walters et al. (2025)**: largest GWAS ever
- **70,000+ ADHD individuals, 650,000+ controls**
- **56 genome-wide significant loci**
- **13 discoverable only through multi-ancestry inclusion**

**Rare variants (Demontis et al., 2025, *Nature*)**:
- **MAP1A** (OR = 13.31)
- **ANO8** (OR = 15.31)
- **ANK2** (OR = 5.55)
- Up to **15-fold increased ADHD risk**

**Epigenetics (Meijer et al., 2025)**: first cell-type-specific methylation map
- ACC: glutamatergic neurons
- Caudate: GABAergic neurons
- Microglia methylation enriched for GWAS risk variants → neuroinflammation

**ADHD clusters genetically with dyslexia** (not autism), forming "attention-and-learning" genetic factor with 49 pleiotropic loci.

PRS currently explains only ~3.6–4% variance, does NOT predict methylphenidate response.

## 4. The gut talks to the brain — and it starts in infancy

**Ahrens et al. (2024, *Cell*)**: 16,440 Swedish children followed 20+ years
- Gut dysbiosis at age 1 — including *Megamonas funiformis* and antibiotic-resistant strains — **precedes ADHD diagnosis by years**
- First large-scale prospective evidence for causal microbiome→ADHD pathway

**Short-chain fatty acid (SCFA) deficiency**: consistent finding across studies
- *Gut Microbes* (2025): distinct microbial profiles for each ADHD subtype
- **Boonchooduang et al. (2025)**: psychostimulants *further reduce* microbial diversity and SCFA

**First adult probiotic RCT (Levy Schwartz et al., 2024)**: 3 months multi-strain
- Significantly decreased hyperactivity
- Improved academic performance in 60 college students

**TRACE study (Huberts-Bosch et al., 2025)**: paradigm shift
- **Healthy diet outperformed elimination diet** (51% vs. 35%)
- Better adherence, sustainability
- Supports Mediterranean-type patterns over restrictive elimination

**Tryptophan-kynurenine pathway**: key mechanistic link
**MADDY trial**: micronutrient supplementation increased butyrate-producing bacteria in responders

## 5. Digital therapeutics reach regulatory milestone for adults

**LumosityRx**: FDA cleared December 2025
- **First prescription digital therapeutic for adult ADHD**
- GAMES trial: 560 adults, double-blind, sham-controlled

**Neurofeedback meta-analysis (Westwood et al., 2025, *JAMA Psychiatry*)**: 38 RCTs, 2,472 participants
- **No significant improvement** on probably-blinded assessments (SMD = 0.04)
- Only standard protocols (SCP, theta/beta) showed small effect (SMD = 0.21)
- European ADHD Guidelines: "did not appear to meaningfully benefit"

**Brain stimulation advances:**
- First tACS RCT (Fu et al., 2025): significant inattention improvement after 20 sessions
- Meta-analysis (Salehinejad et al., 2025): **rTMS effective for inattention** (g = −1.44), **tDCS for inhibitory control** (g = 0.39)
- Lower baseline DLPFC volume predicts greater tDCS response

**AI diagnosis**: 70–95% accuracy across modalities (EEG, MRI, behavioral)
**Wearables**: AUC = 0.844 for ADHD detection; ART-CARMA study monitoring 305 adults for 12 months

## 6. ADHD as a circadian disorder: molecular evidence mounts

**73–78% of adults with ADHD** show delayed sleep-wake cycles

**Luu & Fabiano (2025)**: DLMO delayed ~90 minutes, blunted cortisol, attenuated BMAL1/PER2 expression (PER2 correlates with severity)

**Grigore et al. (2025)**: PPARγ agonist rosiglitazone restored clock gene rhythmicity in ADHD patient fibroblasts

**UK Delphi consensus (Asherson et al., 2025)**:
- 99% agreement: ADHD + delayed sleep onset needs pharmacological access
- 91% consensus for melatonin when non-pharm fails
- Protocol: **0.5 mg melatonin 3h before habitual sleep, advance 1h weekly for 3-4 weeks**

**Counterintuitive (Adamis et al., 2026)**: stimulants associated with *improved* sleep quality

**Mendelian randomization (Xiao et al., 2024)**: causal direction ADHD→RLS (OR = 1.20), not reverse

## 7. CBT leads psychosocial interventions

**Yang et al. (2025)**: network meta-analysis, 37 RCTs — CBT superior across short/long-term outcomes

**Key finding**: **6-session CBT = equally effective as 12-session** (Corrales et al., 2024)

**Digital CBT RCT (Antshel et al., 2026)**: Inflow app, 154 adults
- Significant improvements in inattention (η² = 0.15), QoL

**Exercise (Yang et al., 2025)**: **very large effect size** on inhibitory control (8 RCTs, 372 participants)

**Workplace outcomes (Lauder et al., 2024)**:
- Psychosocial interventions: d = 0.56
- Pharmacological: d = 0.19
- **Medication alone insufficient for functional recovery**

**ADHD coaching**: 60.9% of US coaches started during/after COVID-19 — largely unregulated, no RCT evidence

## 8. Comorbidities: cardiovascular risk, AuDHD, ADHD→PTSD

### Cardiovascular

**Zhang et al. (2024, *JAMA Psychiatry*)**: long-term ADHD medication (>3 years) associated with increased CVD risk, particularly **hypertension and arterial disease**

**Holt et al. (2024, *JACC*)**: dose-dependent 10-year risks
- Stroke: RR 1.2
- Heart failure: RR 1.7
- Composite CVD: RR 1.3

But: Mendelian randomization shows **ADHD itself** genetically linked to CAD and heart failure

### AuDHD

**Thomas Craig et al. (2025)**: 2.4M US claims
- Only **1.7% of ADHD adults** carry ASD diagnosis
- Community studies: ~45% show significant autistic traits
- **Massive underdiagnosis**

### ADHD→PTSD causal pathway

**Brikell et al. (2023, *Am J Psychiatry*)**: combined MR + sibling comparison
- Causal ADHD→PTSD pathway confirmed
- Partially mediated by risk-taking + trauma exposure
- ~28% PTSD patients have ADHD; ~36% ADHD patients have PTSD

### Other

- Combined CBT + LDX = superior for binge eating disorder (Grilo et al., 2025)
- COVID-19 = independent ADHD risk factor (Song et al., 2025)
- ADHD persistence → 2.1× SUD risk, 3.2× depression risk (van der Plas et al., 2026)

## 9. Across the lifespan: mortality, late-onset, dementia, pregnancy

### Mortality

**O'Nions et al. (2025, *Br J Psychiatry*)** — first life-table method:
- Men with ADHD: **73.3 years vs. 80.0** (6.8 years lost)
- Women: **75.2 vs. 83.8** (8.6 years lost)
- ADHD medication reduces mortality gap by ~19%

### Late-onset ADHD

**Sibley et al.**: ~95% apparent late-onset ruled out after rigorous procedures
- Only **0.8% genuine adult onset** (all had complex psychiatric histories)
- But 10 of 12 longitudinal cohorts identified late-onset trajectories (3–17.5%)

### Dementia

**Levine et al. (2023, *JAMA Network Open*)**: 109,218 Israeli adults, 17 years
- ADHD = **2.77-fold increased dementia risk**
- **No increased risk among those on psychostimulants**

**Berberat et al. (2025)**: increased brain iron + elevated NfL in adult ADHD
**Leffa et al. (2025)**: ADHD PRS associates with brain hypometabolism + p-tau

### Pregnancy

**Bang Madsen et al. (2025, *Mol Psychiatry*)**: 861,650 Swedish children
- **No increased NDD risk** in offspring exposed in utero after familial confounding
- Exception: **modafinil increases congenital malformation risk**
- Consensus: documented risks very low, treatment should continue when needed

### Intergenerational transmission

Three major studies (Kleppesto, Brikell, Voronin 2024): parent-to-child resemblance = **direct genetic transmission**
- Only ~2% environmental mediation
- Treating parental ADHD benefits parents but doesn't reduce children's ADHD risk

## 10. Society, disparities, Czech context

### TikTok misinformation

**Karasavva et al. (2025)**: <50% of #ADHD TikTok claims align with DSM
**Schiros et al. (2025)**: TikTok misinformation drives treatment-seeking, overburdening assessment

### Diagnosis rates

- US adult ADHD: **6.0% (15.5 million adults)**, ~half diagnosed in adulthood
- Adult women 23–49: diagnoses **doubled 2020–2022**
- Non-Hispanic White ~26% more likely to be diagnosed than Black
- **Black females most underdiagnosed**
- Women diagnosed **4–5 years later** on average
- Original DSM-5 trials: only 21% female

### 🇨🇿 Czech-specific

- **Vňuková et al. (2021)**: Czech adult prevalence **~3–3.5%** (comparable to Western Europe)
- **Gimbach et al. (2024)**: Czech Republic = **one of only 5 European countries where post-COVID ADHD medication fell below predictions** (−4%)
- **Deb et al. (2025)**: rising ADHD incidence in Czech adults documented via National Registry
- Czech ASRS validated (Vňuková et al., 2022)
- Diagnostic infrastructure relies heavily on clinical judgment, limited standardized adult tools

### Economic burden

- US: **$122.8 billion annually**
- Denmark: ~€20,000 per adult per year
- Adults with ADHD lose ~**22 productive work days/year**
- Post-pandemic: nearly 4-fold increase in incident adult diagnoses (BC, Canada)
- UK Independent ADHD Taskforce: predominant issue is **underdiagnosis, not overdiagnosis**

---

## Five most consequential findings for practice

1. **Stimulants work through arousal and reward, not attention** — prioritize sleep assessment before prescribing

2. **Mortality data is stark**: 6.8–8.6 years lost, reducible by ~19% with medication, potentially protective against dementia

3. **Glutamate emerging** alongside dopamine/norepinephrine, with developmental trajectory predicting persistence vs. remission

4. **Gut microbiome**: infant dysbiosis precedes diagnosis by years, SCFA deficiency consistent finding

5. **Brief CBT (6 sessions) works**; psychosocial interventions outperform medication for workplace outcomes — multimodal treatment essential
